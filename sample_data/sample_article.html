Sample Neurocritical Care Article

This open educational page summarizes high-yield neuro ICU topics for teaching and board review. The goal is to identify common, high-stakes management decisions without reproducing a full copyrighted chapter.

Status Epilepticus

Status epilepticus is a common neurocritical emergency because delayed treatment increases neuronal injury, mortality, and mechanical ventilation exposure. First-line therapy should be delivered rapidly, and clinicians should escalate promptly when seizures continue after the initial antiseizure medication.

A major pitfall is repeating small benzodiazepine doses instead of moving into a structured algorithm. Refractory status epilepticus requires continuous EEG, hemodynamic planning, and rescue therapies when standard escalation fails.

Intracranial Pressure Management

Intracranial pressure crises are high-stakes because herniation causes irreversible neurologic injury. Management should focus on head positioning, sedation, osmotherapy, and cerebral perfusion pressure targets that fit the bedside examination.

Avoid prolonged hyperventilation except as a short bridge during acute deterioration. Guideline-style targets and decision points are especially testable for boards and fellowship education.

ECMO Neuromonitoring

ECMO patients with neurologic injury represent a less common but high-risk population. Advanced neuromonitoring, anticoagulation tradeoffs, and tertiary-center rescue planning push this topic toward the expert tier even when it appears later in a review article.
